Overview

Problem

After a loss, people face emotional and practical challenges. Users need a way to find trusted resources to manage funeral planning, legal matters, and other related tasks, as grief combined with urgent responsibilities makes it difficult to locate and coordinate the necessary services and support.

Success Metrics: Faster service coordination, improved task completion, and positive feedback showing users feel supported and find value in the app.

Solution

We propose a user-friendly app offering personalized bereavement support with local service directories, educational resources, 24/7 support, family coordination tools and secured document management.

Conclusion

The card sort workshop yielded surprising results that challenged my initial assumptions. Before conducting it with participants, I had carefully prepared by running a self-test, refining the card set from 40 to 30 items, and simplifying the wording. I chose a hybrid card sort approach, providing predefined categories while allowing participants to create their own if needed.

The results revealed unexpected patterns. One participant created a "Dedicated Chat Space" category for the "Chat" card, while another made a separate "Task Delegation" category due to unfamiliarity with the term. Many cards proved more versatile than anticipated, fitting logically into multiple categories. For example, the "Favorited Collections of places, articles, etc." card was frequently placed under "My Profile," though I had intended it for the "Home Dashboard" to ensure easy access.

The "My Profile" cards presented another interesting case. While I expected the "My__" prefix to clearly signal their category, the results varied significantly between groups. In the first group, 8 of 14 participants categorized them as intended, but in the second group, 6 of 14 participants placed them elsewhere.

This experience has led me to consider running an open card sort in the future. Though I initially chose to provide categories to guide participants, given the unique nature of my app concept, the results demonstrated that even careful preparation can't prevent our assumptions from being challenged.

05 Test

Return to our users for feedback.

Usability Testing

Goal: Assess user interactions with Soulace, identify pain points, and gather feedback to refine its design. We aim to ensure the app is intuitive and supportive, focusing on seamless service searches, resource access, and account creation.

Test Objectives

  1. Analyze the ease of account creation and setup, ensuring the process feels straightforward and welcoming.
  2. Determine how efficiently users can find and access local services, such as funeral homes, legal offices and support organizations.
  3. Assess how easily users can navigate core features, such as adding a new task, schedule an event and upload documents to the vaults.
  4. Collect feedback on design, functionality and the overall impressions to align the apps experience with user needs during challenging times.

Methodology: The study will be conducted as moderated remote tests. Sessions will be 30 minutes. Test will start off with a brief introduction, a moment to gain rapport, task instructions and performance with Soulace conducted on a mobile prototype through Figma and a debriefing.

Usability Test Report

Test Report Introduction:

This report outlines findings from the usability testing conducted for Soulace. The goal of the test was to evaluate the app’s key features, assess its overall usability, and gather feedback from participants. By analyzing their experiences, we aim to identify areas for improvement and ensure that Soulace provides meaningful support during difficult times. Below are 5 main issues we came across and the solutions we implemented.

Issue #1: Font and Icon size / spacing (High)

  • Suggested change: Increase font sizes throughout the app and add more spacing between icons in the search results to reduce the likelihood of “fat-finger errors”.
  • Evidence: While most participants were able to click the icons accurately, it was noticeable that they had to focus more carefully to ensure they selected the correct one, both visually and physically.

Issue #2: Layout and Organization (Medium)

  • Suggested change: Adjust the layout of the Resources and Support pages, as some participants found it unclear which content belonged on each page.
  • Evidence: When asked about navigating grief-related information, participants expressed confusion about distinguishing content between the Resources and Support pages. One participant suggested moving the “Community Support” section to the top of the Support page for better visibility and hierarchy.

Issue #3: Featured Collections section consistency (High)

  • Suggested change: Clarify this section to ensure users understand that its content remains consistent across all screens..
  • Evidence: While participants grasped the general purpose of the section, some assumed its contents were specific to the screen they were viewing. For example, they believed the articles displayed on the home screen differed from those in the same section on the Resources screen, though the content is actually identical.

Issue #4: Prototype Functionality (High)

  • Suggested change: Increase the functional accuracy of prototypes in key areas.
  • Evidence: Participants attempted to scroll and interact with buttons that were not clickable, leading to some confusion. While prototypes don’t need to be fully functional, making interactive elements more responsive in test-specific areas would improve the testing experience and provide more accurate user feedback.

Conclusion

In conclusion, creating Soulace has been a meaningful journey. An idea rooted from a personal experience, the inspiration to create Soulace came from a genuine desire to offer meaningful support to those navigating the challenge of loss. The mission was to craft a supportive, empathetic space where individuals navigating loss could find comfort, clarity and strength.

In a time when burdens feel overwhelming, Soulace stands as a tool to ease responsibilities, reduce stress and provide a sense of solace. The importance of this work lies in its ability to transform a difficult experience into one that feels guided, supported and a little less isolating. Soulace is more than an app, its a companion for the healing journey we all will experience at some point during our lifetime.
